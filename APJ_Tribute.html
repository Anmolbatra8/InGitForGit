"The best way to predict the future is to create it."



CHILDHOOD

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadui>, India. He grew up in a modest family and had a strong passion for learning and education from a young age.
  • He always had a dream to fly and beleived that he will do so one day. Interestingly , He became the first Person to Fly from his town Later !
  • His first Income was by doing work of Collecting Newspapers which were dumped from trains [due to Onset of the 2nd World war] ,He Helped his elder brother by doing that
  • His early years shaped his determination and dedication to excel in the field of science and make significant contributions to the nation
  • Some of the most influential teachers in his life were :Sivasubramania Iyer who taught him Mathematics,encouraged him to study further and guided the young mind.
  • His physics teacher,Lakshmi Narayanan , who ignited his passion for science and inspired him to pursue a career in research and innovation.






EDUCATION

  • In his school years, Kalam had average grades but was described as a bright and hardworking student who had a strong desire to learn.
    He spent hours on his studies, especially Mathematics.
  • After completing his education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram, Kalam went on to attend Saint Joseph's College , Tiruchirappalli,
    from where he graduated in physics in 1954.
  • He moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology. He narrowly missed achieving his dream of becoming a fighter pilot
    , as he placed ninth in qualifiers, and only eight positions were available in the IAF.
  • As stated in his biography,he didn't had 100rs required for admission in the college So his sister, Sold her bangles for his admission






CAREER

  • APJ Abdul Kalam began his career as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) in India.
  • He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, the SLV-III.
  • Kalam served as the Chief Project Coordinator for the Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, establishing India as a nuclear-armed nation.
  • In 1999, he became the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • Kalam's contributions to the field of missile technology earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India."
  • In 2002, he was elected as the 11th President of India, serving from 2002 to 2007, and became popularly known as the "People's President" for his down-to-earth approach and inspiring leadership.



SETBACKS

  • Rejection in early years : Kalam faced initial rejection in his aspiration to become a fighter pilot due to his height,
    but he shifted his focus to a career in aeronautical engineering.
  • Failure of SLV-III : Despite the initial setback of the failed launch of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle,
    Kalam and his team persevered, leading to subsequent successful launches.
  • Criticism of nuclear tests: The Pokhran-II nuclear tests faced international criticism and sanctions,
    but Kalam remained resolute in his belief in India's national security and the importance of its nuclear capabilities.
  • Unsuccessful presidential candidacy : Kalam's aspiration for a second term as President faced political challenges and
    lack of support, leading to his withdrawal; however, he continued to contribute significantly through his engagement with the youth
